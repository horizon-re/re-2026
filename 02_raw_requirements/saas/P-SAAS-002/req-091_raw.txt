Functional Specification Document: Agentic AI Support Platform1. System OverviewThis document outlines the functional specifications for an Agentic AI Support Platform designed to enhance customer service by integrating autonomous AI agents with human support personnel. The platform aims to create a seamless and efficient support experience, leveraging the strengths of both AI and human capabilities. The architecture is predicated on a collaborative model where AI agents handle a significant portion of customer interactions, escalating to human agents for complex or sensitive issues. This hybrid approach ensures scalability, reduces response times for common queries, and allows human agents to focus on situations requiring empathy and nuanced understanding.1Agentic AI systems possess inherent flexibility and the capacity for dynamic interaction with other AI components within the platform.4 Unlike traditional, narrowly focused AI models, these agents are designed to comprehend overarching objectives, formulate multi-step action plans, evaluate various strategies to achieve goals, adapt to changing circumstances in real-time, and collaborate effectively across different tasks.5 This level of sophistication enables AI agents to operate with a considerable degree of autonomy, independently executing tasks and making decisions without requiring constant human oversight.6 The platform's architecture will support this autonomous behavior while ensuring seamless integration with human agents when necessary.The platform will likely adopt a hybrid architecture, strategically employing both smaller, more efficient AI models (SLMs) for handling routine, task-oriented queries and larger language models (LLMs) for addressing complex issues that demand extensive reasoning and a deep understanding of context.2 This balanced approach is essential for managing the diverse spectrum of customer inquiries effectively and optimizing resource utilization. By assigning simpler, repetitive tasks to SLMs, the platform can conserve computational resources and ensure swift response times for common questions, while reserving the more powerful LLMs and human agents for scenarios that require their advanced capabilities.2A critical aspect of the platform's architecture is the seamless integration of AI agents with human support agents. The system will be designed to facilitate various interaction scenarios, including instances where AI agents handle initial customer engagements, gather essential information, and then transfer the conversation to a human agent for more specialized assistance. Conversely, the platform will also enable AI agents to assist human agents by providing real-time access to relevant information, suggesting appropriate responses, and automating follow-up tasks, thereby enhancing the efficiency and effectiveness of human support personnel.3 The mechanism for transferring conversations between AI and human agents must be carefully designed to ensure a smooth transition and maintain the continuity of the interaction, preventing customer frustration.8The distinct yet complementary roles of agentic AI and human support agents within the platform are summarized in the following table:Feature/CapabilityAgentic AI Support AgentHuman Support AgentInitial InteractionHandles many common queries, gathers initial informationHandles escalated or complex queriesTask ExecutionAutomates repetitive tasks, provides proactive supportProvides empathetic support, handles nuanced situations requiring human judgmentDecision MakingMakes autonomous decisions within defined parametersMakes complex decisions, handles exceptions and policy interpretationsLearning & ImprovementContinuously learns from interactions to improve performanceProvides feedback to refine AI models and workflowsHandoff InitiationDetects need for human intervention based on complexity, sentiment, or predefined rulesReceives handoffs, provides specialized supportAssistance to Human AgentProvides real-time information, suggests responses, automates follow-up tasksLeverages AI insights to improve efficiency and personalize interactions furtherThis table illustrates the intended division of labor and the collaborative synergy between AI and human agents, highlighting the platform's hybrid nature and its focus on delivering comprehensive and efficient customer support.2. Conversation ManagementThe effective management of customer conversations is paramount to the success of the Agentic AI Support Platform. This section details the mechanisms for message routing, session state persistence, and context carry-over, ensuring a fluid and coherent customer experience.The platform will incorporate an intelligent message routing system designed to direct customer inquiries to the most suitable agent, whether AI or human, based on a comprehensive understanding of the query's nature.9 This routing logic will be tightly integrated with the Intent Detection Engine (detailed in Section 3), allowing the system to analyze the content and intent of customer messages to determine the optimal destination. AI routing capabilities can categorize requests based on sentiment and topic by employing Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques.9 Furthermore, the platform will leverage LLM-powered routing, which analyzes the entire context of user inputs to facilitate more nuanced and accurate routing decisions.10 The system may also utilize a hybrid routing approach, combining vector-based methods with LLM escalation to handle a wide range of inquiries effectively.12 The Routing AI agent will have the capability to operate in either Conversational mode, where it acknowledges the customer's intent before routing, or Silent mode, where routing occurs seamlessly without explicit dialogue.13 The flexibility of this routing system is crucial for accommodating various communication channels and adapting to the evolving needs of customers, while also considering factors such as agent availability, specific skill sets, and customer priority.To ensure a consistent and uninterrupted customer experience, the platform must maintain session state, remembering previous interactions and user data throughout the entirety of a conversation.14 This persistence of session state will remain consistent regardless of whether the customer is interacting with an AI agent or a human agent. The platform can employ techniques such as using HTTP cookies to store a unique session ID on the client-side, coupled with server-side storage to persist the associated user data across multiple requests.16 For scenarios involving different types of connections, such as those utilizing the MQTT protocol, the platform will support persistent sessions, allowing clients to maintain their subscriptions and message states even across reconnections.17 Load balancers within the platform's infrastructure may also utilize cookie-based or IP address-based persistence mechanisms to ensure that a client is consistently routed to the same backend server for the duration of their session.18 Maintaining session state is essential for preventing repetitive questioning, preserving the context of ongoing conversations across different agents, and enabling more personalized interactions based on the customer's interaction history and preferences.A critical requirement for the platform is the seamless carry-over of context when a conversation is transferred between an AI agent and a human agent, or vice versa.7 This context must encompass the complete history of the interaction, including all previous messages exchanged, any information that has already been gathered by the AI agent, and the intent that has been identified. This transfer of context will be facilitated through dedicated mechanisms within the handoff API (detailed in Section 6) and by ensuring that all relevant contextual data is stored and accessible through the session state. Larger language models possess the inherent ability to retain conversational context, building upon previous exchanges to maintain coherence and understanding.20 The design of chatbot context management involves the fundamental principle of remembering and effectively utilizing past interactions to inform current and future engagements.19 Ensuring robust context carry-over is paramount to avoid the common frustration experienced by customers when they have to repeat their issues or information to a different agent.8 By providing human agents with a comprehensive understanding of the customer's prior interaction with the AI, the platform aims to enhance agent efficiency and deliver more relevant and effective support without burdening the customer with redundant explanations.3. Intent Detection EngineAt the core of the Agentic AI Support Platform lies the Intent Detection Engine, a critical component responsible for deciphering the underlying purpose behind customer inquiries. This section outlines the specific requirements for this engine, including the types of inputs it will process, the criteria for classifying intents based on confidence levels, and the data necessary for training the model to achieve high accuracy.The Intent Detection Engine will be designed to accept and process a variety of customer input modalities, including text-based queries, spoken language (in the event that voice support is implemented within the platform), and potentially additional contextual information that can aid in understanding the customer's goal.21 This contextual data might include the specific page or section of the platform the user is currently interacting with, or a history of their recent interactions. The engine will employ Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to analyze these diverse input types, extracting meaningful information and identifying the customer's intent.21 Furthermore, the engine should be capable of recognizing and processing utterances that contain multiple intents, allowing for a more nuanced understanding of complex customer needs.13To ensure the reliability and accuracy of intent classification, the engine will utilize confidence scores, which represent the model's certainty in the detected intent.23 Classification thresholds will be established to strike an optimal balance between the accuracy of the predictions (precision) and the engine's ability to identify all relevant intents (recall).23 A higher confidence threshold will prioritize accuracy, meaning that only intents with a high probability of being correct will be accepted, thus reducing the risk of misclassification. Conversely, a lower threshold will allow for the acceptance of more potential intents, which can be beneficial in scenarios where capturing a broader range of user needs is more important than strict precision. The specific confidence threshold for each intent may need to be dynamically adjusted based on the nature of the intent itself and the potential consequences of an incorrect classification.23 Platforms like Dialogflow utilize both rule-based grammar matching and machine learning (ML) matching algorithms to score intents, employing a confidence score threshold to determine the best match.24 Similarly, Teneo provides the functionality to set a global confidence threshold for intent classification.23The performance and effectiveness of the Intent Detection Engine are heavily reliant on the quality and comprehensiveness of the data used to train its machine learning model.25 The model will require a diverse and representative dataset of customer queries, each accurately labeled with its corresponding intent. This training data should encompass a wide spectrum of user expressions, including variations in phrasing, common linguistic errors such as misspellings and grammatical mistakes, and inquiries of varying complexity.27 To maintain and enhance the model's accuracy over time, it will be necessary to continuously update and augment the training data with new examples and feedback from real-world interactions.25 Various sources can contribute to this training dataset, including publicly available datasets, custom data derived from the platform's own chat logs, and conversational data gathered from other customer interactions.26 Benchmarking datasets such as CLINC150 and BANKING77 are also available to evaluate the performance of intent classification models.304. Real-Time AnalyticsThe Agentic AI Support Platform will incorporate robust real-time analytics capabilities to provide immediate insights into the performance of the support system and the satisfaction of customers.31 This section details the key metrics that will be tracked, the essential components of the analytics dashboard, and the frequency at which the data will be refreshed to ensure timely and actionable information.The platform will monitor a range of critical customer support metrics in real-time, offering a dynamic view of the system's effectiveness. These metrics will include Response Time, such as First Response Time (the time taken for the initial response to a customer inquiry) and Average Reply Time (the average time between subsequent replies).32 Resolution Rate will also be tracked, encompassing First Contact Resolution (the percentage of issues resolved in the customer's initial contact) and Overall Resolution Rate (the percentage of all issues resolved within a defined period).32 Customer satisfaction will be measured through Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) scores, gathered via surveys and feedback mechanisms 31, and Customer Effort Score (CES), which quantifies the ease of the customer's interaction with the platform.32 Additionally, the platform will track Net Promoter Score (NPS) to gauge customer loyalty and their likelihood to recommend the service.31 Ticket Volume by Channel will provide insights into the distribution of inquiries across different support channels such as chat, email, and phone.31 Agent Performance Metrics, including Average Handle Time, Resolution Rate per agent, and CSAT per agent, will be monitored for both human and AI agents.31 Specific metrics related to AI agent performance, such as AI Agent Usage and Resolution Rate, as well as the Handoff Rate (the frequency with which AI agents escalate conversations), will also be tracked to assess the effectiveness and efficiency of the AI component of the platform. Monitoring these key metrics in real-time will provide invaluable data for understanding the platform's overall performance, identifying areas that require improvement, and making informed decisions to optimize customer service delivery.The insights derived from these real-time metrics will be presented through a comprehensive analytics dashboard.46 This dashboard will feature several key components designed to provide a clear and actionable overview of support performance. Real-time KPI displays will showcase the current values of the most critical metrics, offering an immediate snapshot of the system's status.42 Trend charts will visualize how these metrics are evolving over time, enabling stakeholders to identify patterns and assess the impact of changes or interventions.31 Agent performance leaderboards will provide a comparative view of both human and AI agent productivity and quality.48 Channel-specific breakdowns will illustrate performance across the various support channels, allowing for targeted analysis and optimization.42 The dashboard will also offer customizable views and filters, empowering users to focus on specific time periods, channels, or individual agents as needed.31 Furthermore, the system will incorporate alerting capabilities, automatically notifying designated personnel when key metrics deviate from predefined thresholds, enabling proactive intervention to address potential issues.32 A well-designed analytics dashboard will transform raw data into readily understandable and actionable information, facilitating data-driven decision-making and continuous improvement of the customer support operations.To ensure that the analytics dashboard provides timely and relevant information, the underlying data will be refreshed in near real-time.49 The specific frequency of data refresh will be determined by the nature of the data sources and the desired level of granularity for monitoring different metrics. For critical, time-sensitive metrics such as the number of active conversations and agent response times, a rapid refresh rate, potentially every few minutes or even seconds in premium configurations, will be implemented.49 For metrics that are less volatile or require monitoring over longer periods, a less frequent refresh cadence may be sufficient. Platforms like Power BI offer premium workspaces that can support refresh rates as low as one second for demanding real-time analytics scenarios 49, while other platforms like Salesforce allow for both manual and automatic dashboard refreshes.51 The goal is to provide support teams and managers with the most current information possible, enabling them to react swiftly to emerging trends, manage workloads effectively, and ensure that service level agreements are consistently met.5. Agentic AI WorkflowsThe agentic AI support agents within the platform will operate through defined workflows that govern their interactions and responsibilities. This section details the triggers for proactive customer outreach, the rules that dictate when a conversation should be escalated to a human agent, and the API endpoints that facilitate the seamless transfer of conversations.Agentic AI agents will be capable of initiating proactive outreach to customers based on a set of predefined triggers.52 These triggers will be designed to identify situations where proactive engagement can enhance the customer experience or prevent potential issues. For example, the AI might detect that a user is encountering difficulties while navigating a particular feature of the platform, triggering an offer of assistance or a helpful tutorial.52 The system could also proactively remind customers about upcoming subscription renewals or payment deadlines.52 Based on a customer's past interactions or stated preferences, the AI agent could offer personalized recommendations for products or services.53 Furthermore, the AI will be equipped to recognize signs of user struggle, such as repeated attempts to complete a task or prolonged inactivity on a help page, prompting a proactive offer of support.52 This proactive approach aims to improve customer satisfaction by anticipating needs and addressing potential issues before they escalate, ultimately reducing the overall demand on support resources.The platform will implement a clearly defined set of escalation rules that determine when an AI agent should transfer a conversation to a human support agent.7 These rules will be based on several factors, including the complexity of the customer's issue; if the AI agent is unable to understand or effectively resolve the query, it will escalate the conversation.7 The sensitivity of the topic will also be considered; issues involving personal or confidential information, or customer complaints, may require the nuanced handling of a human agent.61 The AI will also monitor the customer's sentiment, and if frustration or anger is detected, the conversation may be escalated to a human agent who can provide empathetic support.7 Certain predefined intents, particularly those involving complex transactions or policy exceptions, might be configured to always be routed to a human agent.61 To prevent customer frustration, the platform will also implement rules based on the number of interaction turns; if the AI agent is unable to reach a resolution within a specified number of exchanges, the conversation will be escalated.63 These clear escalation protocols ensure that customers receive the appropriate level of support and that human agents are available to handle situations that require their expertise and judgment.To facilitate the transfer of conversations between AI and human agents, the platform will provide a set of well-defined API endpoints.70 These API endpoints will manage several key aspects of the handoff process. They will enable the AI agent to initiate the handoff, signaling to the system the need for human intervention. Crucially, these endpoints will also handle the transfer of the complete conversation context, ensuring that the human agent receiving the handoff has access to the full history of the interaction between the customer and the AI agent. Additionally, the API will update the status of the conversation within the platform to reflect the change in agent ownership, ensuring proper tracking and workflow management. A robust and well-designed API is essential for creating a seamless and efficient handoff experience, minimizing disruption for the customer and providing human agents with all the necessary information to effectively continue the support interaction.6. Human-AI HandoffThe seamless transition of customer interactions between AI and human agents is a critical aspect of the Agentic AI Support Platform. This section specifies the requirements for this handoff process, focusing on the user interface indicators, the format in which conversation transcripts will be transferred, and the necessary audit trail information.The user interface of the platform will clearly indicate to the customer whether they are currently interacting with an AI agent or a human agent.76 This distinction can be achieved through the use of distinct visual cues such as different avatars for AI and human agents, clearly displayed agent names or labels, or other intuitive design elements. When a handoff occurs, the UI must dynamically update to reflect the change in the type of agent with whom the customer is now communicating. Additionally, the platform may employ notifications to inform the user explicitly that they are being connected with a human support agent.78 Providing this transparency is essential for managing customer expectations and fostering trust in the support process. Clear visual indicators will help customers understand the nature of their interaction and adjust their communication accordingly.When a conversation is transferred from an AI agent to a human agent, it is imperative that the human agent receives the complete transcript of the prior interaction in a clear and easily readable format.79 The transcript format should include detailed timestamps for each message, clear identifiers indicating whether the message was sent by the AI or the customer, and the full content of every message exchanged. This transcript should be readily accessible to the human agent within their standard interface, ensuring that they have all the necessary context to understand the issue and continue the conversation effectively without requiring the customer to repeat information. Providing the full interaction history not only saves the customer time and frustration but also enables the human agent to provide more informed and relevant support.The platform will maintain a comprehensive audit trail of all handoff events that occur between AI and human agents.88 This audit trail will record specific details about each handoff, including the precise time at which the transfer took place, a unique identifier for the conversation, the type of agent that initiated the handoff (specifically indicating it was an AI agent), the identifier of the human agent who received the conversation, and, if available, the reason or trigger that prompted the handoff. The audit trail may also include other relevant system information related to the handoff event. Maintaining such a detailed record of all handoffs is crucial for several reasons. It allows for effective monitoring of the platform's performance, providing data that can be analyzed to identify trends and areas for improvement in both AI and human agent workflows. The audit trail also ensures accountability and provides a valuable resource for reporting and compliance purposes. By tracking the frequency, reasons, and outcomes of handoffs, the platform administrators can gain valuable insights into the effectiveness of the AI agents and the efficiency of the overall support process.ConclusionsThe Agentic AI Support Platform aims to revolutionize customer service by creating a synergistic partnership between autonomous AI agents and skilled human support personnel. This Functional Specification Document has outlined the key functionalities required for the platform, covering the system architecture, conversation management, intent detection, real-time analytics, AI workflows, and the critical human-AI handoff process. By implementing these specifications, the platform will be equipped to deliver efficient, personalized, and high-quality customer support, leveraging the speed and scalability of AI for routine tasks while ensuring human expertise is readily available for complex and sensitive issues. The emphasis on seamless context transfer and transparent communication will contribute to a positive customer experience and enhance overall support operations.# Functional Specification Document: Agentic AI Support Platform1. Human-AI HandoffThe seamless transition of customer interactions between AI and human agents is a critical aspect of the Agentic AI Support Platform. This section specifies the requirements for this handoff process, focusing on the user interface indicators, the format in which conversation transcripts will be transferred, and the necessary audit trail information.The user interface of the platform will clearly indicate to the customer whether they are currently interacting with an AI agent or a human agent.76 This distinction can be achieved through the use of distinct visual cues such as different avatars for AI and human agents, clearly displayed agent names or labels, or other intuitive design elements. When a handoff occurs, the UI must dynamically update to reflect the change in the type of agent with whom the customer is now communicating. Additionally, the platform may employ notifications to inform the user explicitly that they are being connected with a human support agent.78 Providing this transparency is essential for managing customer expectations and fostering trust in the support process. Clear visual indicators will help customers understand the nature of their interaction and adjust their communication accordingly.When a conversation is transferred from an AI agent to a human agent, it is imperative that the human agent receives the complete transcript of the prior interaction in a clear and easily readable format.79 The transcript format should include detailed timestamps for each message, clear identifiers indicating whether the message was sent by the AI or the customer, and the full content of every message exchanged. This transcript should be readily accessible to the human agent within their standard interface, ensuring that they have all the necessary context to understand the issue and continue the conversation effectively without requiring the customer to repeat information. Providing the full interaction history not only saves the customer time and frustration but also enables the human agent to provide more informed and relevant support.The platform will maintain a comprehensive audit trail of all handoff events that occur between AI and human agents.88 This audit trail will record specific details about each handoff, including the precise time at which the transfer took place, a unique identifier for the conversation, the type of agent that initiated the handoff (specifically indicating it was an AI agent), the identifier of the human agent who received the conversation, and, if available, the reason or trigger that prompted the handoff. The audit trail may also include other relevant system information related to the handoff event. Maintaining such a detailed record of all handoffs is crucial for several reasons. It allows for effective monitoring of the platform's performance, providing data that can be analyzed to identify trends and areas for improvement in both AI and human agent workflows. The audit trail also ensures accountability and provides a valuable resource for reporting and compliance purposes. By tracking the frequency, reasons, and outcomes of handoffs, the platform administrators can gain valuable insights into the effectiveness of the AI agents and the efficiency of the overall support process.ConclusionsThe Agentic AI Support Platform aims to revolutionize customer service by creating a synergistic partnership between autonomous AI agents and skilled human support personnel. This Functional Specification Document has outlined the key functionalities required for the platform, covering the system architecture, conversation management, intent detection, real-time analytics, AI workflows, and the critical human-AI handoff process. By implementing these specifications, the platform will be equipped to deliver efficient, personalized, and high-quality customer support, leveraging the speed and scalability of AI for routine tasks while ensuring human expertise is readily available for complex and sensitive issues. The emphasis on seamless context transfer and transparent communication will contribute to a positive customer experience and enhance overall support operations.
